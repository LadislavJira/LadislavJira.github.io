My Story

Born and raised in Liberec ( born13.8.2004... a Friday!), technology and computers have been my world for as long as I can remember. What started as playing around on dad's PC evolved into a serious pursuit of software engineering. My path led me from specialized IT studies at Secondary Technical School in Liberec (graduated 2024) to the Technical University of Liberec, where I am currently specialing in Intelligent Systems to explore the depths of Computer Science, AI, Cybersecurity and Software Engineering.

Unlike many students, I didn't wait for graduation to start my career. Since 2022, I have been working at Skeleton Software, transitioning from an intern to a Junior Developer. This hands-on experience with C#, JavaScript, MAUI, .NET, Tizen, and webOS has taught me that software development is as much about solving puzzles as it is about writing code.

Core Values & Strengths

Combining Theory and Practice

I don't just study concepts, but I also implement them. Balancing my full-time university studies with my role as a Junior Developer at Skeleton Software gives me a unique advantage I would say. I bring academic experience into my professional code, and practical, industry-standard efficiency back into my university projects.

The "Puzzle-Solver" Mindset

To me, a bug in asynchronous JavaScript or a rendering issue on a smart TV isn't a frustration, it's like a puzzle to me. My background in mathematics and my naturally analytical nature drive me to dig deep into the root cause of a problem rather than just applying a quick, temporary patch.

Detail-Oriented Precision

My very first job at Magna Exteriors taught me that even millimeters matter. I carry that exact same precision into my software development. I take personal responsibility for my work, ensuring that my C# or .NET code is clean, optimized, and reliable before it ever reaches production.

Meaningful Collaboration

I might not be the loudest or most talkative person in the room, but I am the one you can always rely on. Just as I value a tighter group of close friends in my personal life, I thrive in focused, collaborative development teams. I enjoy mentoring classmates in complex logic and actively absorbing feedback from senior developers.

Cross-Disciplinary Thinking

Because my interests span from history and geography to astronomy, I don't look at code in a vacuum. I try to understand the broader context of the software I build. Understanding how things evolved in the past helps me design applications that make sense for the users of tomorrow.

Seamless Adaptability

The tech stack I use today might change tomorrow, and I am entirely comfortable with that. Jumping between React, MAUI, and even specialized TV operating systems like Tizen or webOS has taught me how to learn fast. I treat every unknown technology as an exciting opportunity to expand my toolkit.
